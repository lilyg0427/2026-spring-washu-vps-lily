I like this idea that question of moral techonology should be on both the developers/creators and the users of the technology. When making technology, you have to understand how it will affect the world once it's out for use, whether it will make lives easier or harder. Users of the technology have to also consider these quesitons, though, as the technology-makers cannot oversee every negative effect and every negative way people will use their technology. I also like some of the questions that ask about dependence on the technology, like if you can live without it. I think this is an interesting question to ask, because in reality, we can live, and have lived, without nearly every technology. It's not necessarily wrong to depend on a technology instead of doing things the "old-fashioned way," but it's important to dissect if you are able to understand how to live without the technology, and whether you feel extremely uncomfortable without the technology, which means you are too reliant to technology.
