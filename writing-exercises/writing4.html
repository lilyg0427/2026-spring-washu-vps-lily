I appreciate the practice of writing letters and sending postcards from the past. I think handwritten notes to people are very heartfelt and I like how personal it is and how you have to take your time with it. Today, you can send a text message or call someone to hear from them when they’re away, which is nice in that you can talk whenever and always stay connected. However, it can feel impersonal when it’s coming through a device, and you don’t have the physicality that a letter has. I also really appreciate the collection of things that are coming with letter- writing, whether it is collecting well-designed postcards or collecting stamps to mail out those letters. Both of these have always been extremely intentionally designed, but often overlooked, especially as they become less and less relevant today. These are also practical displays of art, as they serve another purpose along with looking nice, which I appreciate since I like to decorate with a practical purpose.

The act of writing letters is extremely personal, since you have to take time to think of what you want to say, and may not have a lot of room, and can’t easily erase something without leaving behind some sort of trace. I think it offers us a point of intentional connection with people, since you have to be so deliberate in crafting a letter and sending it. Sending postcards are also a great connection point for when you are really far away, as it allows you stay connected in that person’s life, a direct connection to where they are, while still having a personal note written on it, too. These acts teach us how to truly connect with one another even when it seems futile, because we are far apart, or there’s some sort of obstacle in the way. But writing letters and postcards show that you can still show up for people and let them know you care.
