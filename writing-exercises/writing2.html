Crochet is a human-made process that brings out the best in me whenever I practice it. Not only is it a creative practice, but it’s one that’s away from screens, and can be either for myself or for another person. I believe this brings out the best in me because human connection with others, through gift-giving or favors, is extremely important and makes us all kinder and more thoughtful versions of ourselves. The personal aspect of knowing what another person might want, spending money on supplies and time on the act of making, allows me to really make sure I am considering others and showing them I care about them. In a similar vein, I’m able to do this for myself, when I’m crocheting an item for myself rather than another person. When making for myself, there’s a lot of self-reflection going on between what I want, versus what I need, my stress level, and my free time to devote to it. A large part of the process is to help me relax, since it’s often a repetitive task, while still maintaining aspects that allow me to express myself through design or look.

The act of crochet brings out positive qualities of self-reflection, generosity, and creative expression. Self-reflection and creative expression are positive qualities in my opinion, since they encourage an active mind away from work, school, or analytical thinking that we often do day-to-day. It allows an exercise of your brain that many people don’t use very often, so it’s important to use it when you can. Another positive to crochet is that you have to do it more to get better at it, so it’s a practice for me that I have to learn to be okay with something maybe not looking okay, or if I mess up. It teaches me resilience, especially in something creativity that I don’t have to do for work. I think the combination of being a stress-relief activity that can be for others or for myself, and still allows for creative expression, allows crochet to be a process that brings out the best in me and allows me to uplift others.
