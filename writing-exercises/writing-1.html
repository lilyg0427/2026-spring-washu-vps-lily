One routine I have everyday that helps bring my mood up in the mornings, and set the tone for the rest of the day, is picking out my fun socks. My morning routine usually follows the same structure, of waking up, using the bathroom, then getting changed, etc., so I usually do everything in the same order. I sometimes lightly stress out about my outfit for the day, especially if it’s fall or spring and I don’t know if I should wear pants or shorts or a coat. However, getting to pick out my socks to match my outfit, or go against it, has always been enough to lift my mood and get me ready for the day. I think this small aspect of fun in my life helps me remember that ordinary everyday things can also be exciting and whimsical, and we don’t always have to stick to boring and bland. I also think it helps ground me in the day, as I’ll sometimes match the pattern on the socks to something I’m doing (wearing egg and toast socks when I’m going out to brunch, for example). The fact that I have a large collection to choose from really allows me to feel like I have a lot of freedom and control over my small choices. Even when I have ‘serious’ business, I want to be able to express my personality, and choosing my socks is a great, intentional way of thinking about I will be presented and perceived, and allowing me to express parts of my personality in places that normally wouldn’t allow it.

I think this sock-picking ritual can help us understand that small choices that have become so second-nature and overlooked in society can still have a huge impact on our wellbeing. Many people don’t look at each other’s socks, or don’t put much thought in besides white or black socks, so it becomes another ordinary choice you become complicit in. However, intentionality behind this small choice helps reframe some of your other larger choices, and allows you to appreciate the smaller things in life. I think this is something we really need to come back to, since we as humans can get overwhelmed by life really easily. But bringing a bit of joy back into an everyday choice, like a fun pair of socks, helps us to feel a little more grounded, and a little more whimsical.
