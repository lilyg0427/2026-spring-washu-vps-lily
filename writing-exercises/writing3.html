I prefer the slower or harder way when it comes to logical homework problems, in order to best understand it. Specifically, I think of when I’m coding for my CompSci class, and how I often write code in an inefficient way, rather than the most concise and easier way of coding. I find I have to logic my way through a question first, writing out every step of the process, even if it doesn’t need to be shown. It helps me understand exactly what I’m doing and how to do it before writing it in a way that other people understand. This also applies to a lot of my STEM homework, like for math and physics. I often prefer writing the whole steps of a problem, like manually taking an integral for example instead of using a calculator, because I worry I won’t understand where variables come from and the full process of the solution. I think I prefer this sort of overthinking and overwriting because it aids my learning process. My goal is not to get the right answer, but understand where and how I got to the answer. If I don’t write out everything, I worry I’m sacrificing some valuable thinking and learning that comes from making mistakes or understanding exactly how I process the information.

A lot of students nowadays are really dependent on online platforms and AI when it comes to thinking through problems, and I really want to try to avoid that. I don’t avoid these tools, but I try to use them as a secondary check on my work, rather than having them do the thinking for me. Being intentional in my process allows me to make sure the work I put in will aid me in the future, with any career I choose, as perseverance and clear communication are necessary for almost every job. I think this slower way of working also allows a lot of self-reflection, since I actually see my thought process and every step I take written out in a form. It helps me better understand how I work, so I’m able to improve on that or adjust things based on the project/assignment.
