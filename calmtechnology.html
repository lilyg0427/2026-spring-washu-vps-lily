I found this idea of peripheral and centralized attention when it comes to technology really interesting, especially as it related to typical design principles. When creating analog or digital art, you are often taught to include details that can only be noticed by close inspection, or by far inspection. I feel like this idea of calm technology related to that, as the article describes how technology should exist in your peripheral attention, but have enough interest to easily shift to the center of attention. I also enjoyed the process of computers, as I think the mainframe-personal-ubiquitous computer path can be applied to other simpler technologies. Mainly, it made me think about how things like slang start with a couple people who define it and use it, then it becomes regularly used by everyone, and then either falls off and becomes obsolete, or becomes so ingrained in everyday life that we forget the origin.
