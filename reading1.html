One thing I noticed while reading is that the websites mentioned are extremely distinct and unique, making them much more memorable than restaurant websites that look very general and bland. Each website mentioned had a specific thing that made it stand out, tied to some sort of aspect of the restaurant, like its aesthetic, rotating menu, or customization options. I also thought it was interesting that there was some emphasis placed on the publishing aspect of restaurants, where they can be updated. However, some of the websites they highlight haven’t updated in a while, at least menu wise. This implies that good design might outweigh the need to be updated, since a website can still be memorable without it. I do think restaurant websites have become a little obsolete, with the use of Google or Yelp, or even creating apps instead, which makes me wonder if we’ve switched more to a mobile-focused society instead of an internet or desktop experience.
